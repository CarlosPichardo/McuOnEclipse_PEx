SPI Interface
There are a few things to consider using the the driver and the SPI protocol:
  • Clock edge: component setting to 'falling edge' (CPOL=0, CPHA=0, data is sampled on clock rising edge!)
  • Shift clock idle polarity: low (clock is low if not used)
  • Output pin idle polarity: high (MOSI is high if not used)
  • Send MSB first: yes
  • Verify for the SPI interface that the pins are configured properly (input or output)
  • You need to configure the SPI clock for two speed modes: Low (min 100 kHz and max 400 kHz) and High (max 12 MHz, some cards can take up to 20 MHz)
Board Settings
Below are example settings for using the Display3000 (www.display3000.com) SD Card adapter with the DEMOJM board:
  • MISO: MISO2 on PTB0 (pin 10 on J1), remove jumper Y/PTB0 (accelerometer), to SD card adapter pin 5
  • MOSI: MOSI2 on PTB1 (pin 12 on J1), remove jumper Z/PTB1 (accelerometer), to SD card adapter pin 6
  • SCLK: SPSCK2 on PBT2 (pin 14 on J1), remove jumper POT_EN (potentiometer), to SD card adapter pin 7
  • SS: SS2 on PTB3 (pin 16 on J1), remove jumper X/PTB3 (accelerometer), to SD card adapter pin 8
  • Vdd: (5V) pin 1 on J1 to SD card adapter pin 4
  • GND: pin 3 on J1 to SD card adapter pin 2
  • Connect SD card adapter pin 10 (select) to 5V to have the card always selected
