Component LM61B
Temperatur Sensor LM61B from National Semiconductor
Component Level: Low
Category: CPU External Devices-Sensor
This bean implements a driver to the National Semiconductor device (LM61B).
The LM61B is a precision integrated-circuit temperature sensor that can sense a -30°C to +100°C temperature range.
The LM61B output voltage is linearly proportional to Celsius (Centigrade) temperature (+10 mV/°C) and has a DC offset of +600 mV.
The nominal output voltage of the LM61B ranges from +300 mV to +1600 mV for a -30°C to +100°C temperature range.
The LM61B is calibrated to provide accuracies of ±2.0°C at room temperature and ±3°C over the full -25°C to +85°C temperature range.
The LM61B linear output, +600 mV offset, and factory calibration simplifies the external circuitry required in a single supply environment where reading negative temperatures is required.
Shutdown capability for the LM61B is intrinsic because its inherent low power consumption allows it to be powered directly from the output of many logic gates.
